Why Rome?

Rome, the Eternal City

In the Aeneid, an epic by Virgil, the poet describes Rome this way: “Imeprium sine fine,” which translates in English as “an empire without end.” This prideful attitude toward the city in this phrase is believed to come from the citizens feeling that even if empires rose and fell, the city would last forever.

This beautiful town boasts an ancient history long more than two and a half thousand years, stately buildings of the past, monuments of the greatest Italian artists of all times and countless masterpieces in terms of art, architecture and culture, as well as a unique aura.

ROME FOOD

Here are some of the best food in Rome

Pasta all'Amatriciana

Pasta all'amatriciana is a traditional Italian pasta sauce based on guanciale, pecorino romano cheese, tomato, and, in some variations, onion.

Learn more

Pizza al Taglio

Pizza al taglio is a distinctly Roman style of pizza and features either adorned dough cooked in sheet pans or worked into an oblong shape, then baked. The pizza is served by the slice and is cut with a knife or scissor.

Learn more

Coda alla Vaccinara

Coda alla Vaccinara is a stew made with slow-braised ox-tail with vegetables, tomatoes, red wine, and spices. Coda alla vaccinara (oxtail stew) is considered by many to be the crème de la crème of Roman food.
